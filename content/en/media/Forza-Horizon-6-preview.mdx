## Launch Preparation Guide: How to Get the Most from Week One

A great **Forza Horizon 6 preview** article should do more than hype visuals—it should help you play smarter at launch. Use this checklist to avoid common first-week mistakes.

| Step | Action | Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Choose quality vs performance mode before long sessions | Consistent feel and fewer readjustments |
| 2 | Build 3 starter tunes (grip, drift, all-terrain) | Faster adaptation across map regions |
| 3 | Prioritize road discovery during first sessions | Better fast-travel utility and route knowledge |
| 4 | Save one budget car for urban technical runs | More control in tight city and alley zones |
| 5 | Join convoy sessions early | Quicker social learning and event variety |
| 6 | Bookmark favorite meet locations | Instant community value for your club/friends |

### Recommended First 10-Hour Structure

1. **Hour 1–2:** Follow opening progression and unlock core systems.  
2. **Hour 3–4:** Explore mountain and forest roads; collect discovery progress.  
3. **Hour 5–6:** Test city loops at different times/weather conditions.  
4. **Hour 7–8:** Run drift and PR-focused tuning passes at docks.  
5. **Hour 9–10:** Join or host a convoy meet using one fixed route and one freestyle segment.

This approach lets you sample the full map personality early instead of getting stuck repeating the same easy events.

> **Tip:** Keep one lightly modified “realistic street build” alongside your power builds. It often creates better races and cleaner convoy runs on technical roads.
