## Map and world design: how to race Japan efficiently

Japan is not just a cosmetic backdrop in this release. The world is built around contrast: urban expressways, tight city districts, mountain touge roads, docks, valleys, and seasonal environmental shifts. To progress quickly, rotate your driving activity by terrain type instead of farming one event category.

### Early map strategy (first 8–12 hours)

1. **Drive reconnaissance loops first** around Tokyo and nearby mountain routes.
2. **Tag technical roads** where handling builds outperform raw speed.
3. **Complete qualifiers in mixed zones** to avoid over-specialized tuning too early.
4. **Save high-power builds** for wider circuits and high-speed stretches.
5. **Keep one all-round setup** for exploration and collectible tracking.

| Terrain Type | Best Build Focus | Mistake to Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| **Tokyo streets** | **Balanced accel + braking + quick steering response** | Overbuilding top speed for short corner chains |
| **Mountain passes** | **Grip, suspension control, predictable throttle** | Aggressive power tunes that break traction |
| **Dock/industrial routes** | **Mid-range torque + stable transition handling** | Very stiff setups that punish curbs/obstacles |
| **Open high-speed roads** | **Top speed + aero stability** | Using low-power class builds too long |

Because seasonal shifts are more visible and influential, test your core cars in multiple weather and surface moods as soon as possible. This keeps your tuning sheet flexible for event rotations.

## Campaign progression and wristbands: your optimal path

The new campaign starts you as a tourist, then pushes you through qualifiers and wristbands. This structure is useful if you play intentionally: each phase should have a different objective.

| Progress Phase | Primary Goal | Recommended Focus |
|---|---|---|
| **Tourist Start** | **Join the festival via qualifiers** | Learn roads, unlock practical starter vehicles |
| **Wristband Climb** | **Clear race/events to rank up** | Build 2–3 class-focused garage anchors |
| **Spectacle Gate Events** | **Pass challenge events for next tier** | Practice control, not only speed |
| **Gold Wristband** | **Unlock higher-tier content incl. Legend Island** | Shift toward specialized builds + social events |

### Practical progression checklist

- **Qualifiers first, cosmetics second.** Save heavy cosmetic spending until stable income.
- **Keep class coverage broad.** Maintain at least one reliable build for lower, mid, and performance-heavy categories.
- **Use challenge events as skill tests.** If you fail repeatedly, retune braking and corner exit behavior before adding power.
- **Unlock exploration value.** Collectibles, houses, and landmarks feed long-term progression loops.

A lot of players will rush gold wristband content and hit a tuning wall. Avoid that by reinforcing your handling baseline before investing in top-end performance.

> **Warning:** If your car feels “fast but inconsistent,” fix tire behavior and throttle predictability first. Unstable speed costs more time than moderate power in technical sections.
