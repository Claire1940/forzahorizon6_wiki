## Barn Finds vs. Aftermarket Used Cars

One of the most significant additions to the 2026 gameplay loop is the "Aftermarket" or "Used Car" system. While **Forza Horizon 6 barn finds** typically focus on vintage restoration projects that require time to fix, Aftermarket cars are pre-modified vehicles found abandoned or for sale in the open world.

These vehicles often come with unconventional "Forza Edition" style upgrades that cannot be easily replicated in the standard upgrade shop. For instance, players might stumble upon a Mazda Miata with a unique widebody kit and off-road suspension already installed. This creates a dual-layered hunt: one for the classic restoration projects and another for "ready-to-race" custom builds.

## Expanding Functionality: Barns as Personal Garages

A major community request that has finally been addressed in 2026 is the utility of the barns themselves. In previous titles, once a car was recovered, the barn became a useless landmark. In Forza Horizon 6, barns can be converted into personal, customizable garages.

This new functionality allows players to store not just the original find, but any vehicle of their choice within these hidden spots. The customization engine for these spaces is robust, allowing you to change lighting, floor textures, and decorative props.

### Customization Options for Your Barn
*   **Prop Placement:** Add toolboxes, lifts, and neon signs to create a specific aesthetic.
*   **Creative Hub Integration:** Download garage interior designs created by other players.
*   **Vehicle Display:** Choose which cars from your collection are visible on the "lifts" or in the stalls.
*   **Social Spaces:** Invite friends to your specific barn location to showcase your latest builds in a private setting.

> **💡 Tip:** To unlock the ability to customize a barn, you must first complete the restoration of the specific vehicle associated with that location.

## Restoration and Maintenance

Once a vehicle is recovered, the restoration process begins. In 2026, this is more than just a timer. Players can now influence the speed of restoration by completing "Restoration Challenges" using other cars from the same manufacturer or era. 

For example, if you find a vintage Ferrari, winning races in other Ferrari models will decrease the time the mechanic takes to finish the project. Alternatively, you can spend Credits to "fast-track" the process, though this is often expensive and discouraged for early-game players.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Selling a barn find car on the Auction House is possible once restored, but you cannot "re-find" the car. Make sure you truly want to part with it before confirming the sale!

A: The Nissan Figaro is part of the "Treasure Car" series. You must find the specific photo clue in the Horizon menu and match the background of the photo to an industrial area on the map.
