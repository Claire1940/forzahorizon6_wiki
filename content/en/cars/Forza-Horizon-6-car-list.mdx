## How to evaluate each car in the Forza Horizon 6 car list

A lot of players look at horsepower first. That’s understandable, but it often leads to frustrating builds. For practical performance, evaluate each car by role and consistency.

### 1) Check PI efficiency first
If a car gains huge performance index (PI) from one upgrade path but stays unstable, it might underperform despite “better stats.”

### 2) Match drivetrain to event goals
- AWD: safer traction for mixed and wet routes  
- RWD: stronger drift and high-skill road control  
- FWD: useful in lower classes with corner entry stability

### 3) Look at weight and wheelbase
Long wheelbase can improve high-speed stability; short wheelbase can sharpen rotation but may become twitchy at S1/S2 pace.

### 4) Test three route types
Before locking a tune, run:
- One technical circuit
- One long sprint
- One mixed surface route

This quickly reveals whether a car is truly versatile or only strong in one niche.

| Evaluation Factor | What to Measure | Red Flag | Green Flag |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Launch/Traction** | 0–100 pull and exit grip | Wheelspin in 2nd/3rd gear | Predictable exits under throttle |
| **Mid-Corner Balance** | Steering response at speed | Push/understeer on entry | Neutral or slight controllable rotation |
| **Braking Stability** | High-speed deceleration | Rear instability or lockups | Straight, repeatable braking lines |
| **Surface Adaptability** | Grip on dirt/asphalt changes | Sudden snap over bumps | Composed behavior on transitions |

> **Tip:** Keep two tunes for your most-used cars (grip + power). Seasonal objectives often reward quick switching more than frequent car swapping.

## Event-specific priorities: road, dirt, street, and seasonal playlists

The **Forza Horizon 6 car list** matters most when events force category limits. A car that looks average in free roam can become top-tier in a narrow ruleset.

### Road Racing
Prioritize braking and mid-corner speed over top speed alone. Many players lose time entering corners too aggressively.

### Street Racing
You’ll need stable high-speed response and clean recovery after small impacts or route corrections.

### Dirt Racing
AWD and suspension tuning are usually more important than max horsepower.

### Seasonal Playlists
Plan around restrictions (manufacturer, decade, drivetrain, PI cap). Build “utility cars” that can convert to multiple classes.

| Event Type | Best Car Traits | Common Mistake | Better Approach |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Road** | Corner speed + braking confidence | Chasing top-speed only | Tune for consistency per lap |
| **Street** | Stability + acceleration | Overly stiff race setup | Slightly softer suspension balance |
| **Dirt** | AWD traction + shock control | Road tires on mixed terrain | Use appropriate tire compound |
| **Cross-Country** | Torque + travel + durability | Low ride height builds | Raise ride height for landing control |
| **Seasonal Restricted** | Flexibility under PI limits | One-build-fits-all mentality | Maintain multiple situational tunes |
