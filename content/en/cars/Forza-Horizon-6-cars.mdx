1. **Pick one reliable all-rounder in A class** for road racing.
2. **Add one S1 grip car** for fast circuits and seasonal speed events.
3. **Buy one dedicated off-road truck/SUV** for dirt and cross-country.
4. **Keep one drift-focused RWD car** for drift zone stars and skill chains.

## Secret and Special Variants: How to Treat Them

A lot of attention is on unannounced or lightly documented entries in the **Forza Horizon 6 cars** discussion, including Forza Edition and unusual trims. These can be exciting, but don’t build your entire strategy around them until you can verify unlock paths.

What to do instead:

- **Track by function**, not hype (credit boost, skill multiplier, class strength)
- **Wait for real acquisition methods** (Autoshow, seasonal rewards, wheelspin pools, DLC)
- **Compare base version vs special version** before buying duplicate upgrades
- **Avoid early over-investment** in a car that may be event-locked

> **Warning:** Special-edition demand can distort Auction House prices in early months. If a variant doesn’t unlock a clear gameplay advantage, hold your credits.

A smart method is to maintain a “wishlist tier”:

- **Tier 1:** Immediate event utility
- **Tier 2:** Build diversity and fun
- **Tier 3:** Collector prestige

This keeps your progression steady even if rumored vehicles arrive later than expected.
