## How to Build an Efficient Progression Plan in the Open World

Many players burn out by overfocusing one mode. The best plan for a map-heavy racer is a rotation model: race, explore, tune, repeat.

### Recommended 4-Phase Session Loop

| Phase | Duration | What to Do | Outcome |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Scout** | 20-30 min | Drive unexplored roads and tag points of interest | Better map intelligence |
| **Compete** | 30-45 min | Run 2-4 major events in one region | Credits and unlock momentum |
| **Refine** | 15-20 min | Tune one car for that region’s terrain | Cleaner lap performance |
| **Roam** | 20-30 min | Free drive in changing conditions | Route mastery + enjoyment |

This pacing helps you engage the **forza horizon 6 open world** as a system, not just a race list.

### Tuning Priorities by Terrain

1. **Urban zones:** prioritize braking response and low-speed exit traction  
2. **Mountain routes:** prioritize stability and weight transfer control  
3. **Open roads:** prioritize high-speed confidence over raw top-end  
4. **Mixed-season drives:** prioritize predictable handling under variable grip  

When weather and seasons shift, retest your setups quickly. A tune that dominates in dry urban routes may feel unstable in cooler mountain conditions.
